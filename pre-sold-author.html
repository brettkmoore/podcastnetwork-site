FAQ

Questions We Hear Before People Apply

"Will it actually sound like me?"

Yes, because it is built from your words. Every sentence in your manuscript originates from something you said in an interview. Our editors shape and organize; they do not invent. The result reads like the best, most coherent version of how you already talk about your work.

"What if my podcast appearances don't generate pre-orders?"

The tour is designed to generate pre-orders, but we do not guarantee a specific sales number. No honest program can. What we do guarantee is 15 quality podcast bookings on shows that reach your target audience, a professionally written manuscript, and a published book. The pre-order page is live throughout the tour, and we provide promotional support. Most clients see their first pre-orders within the first two to three episodes.

"I don't have a big audience. Will this still work?"

The podcast tour is your audience-building mechanism, not a prerequisite. You are borrowing the audiences of 15 established shows. You do not need to bring your own following. You are building one in real time as the tour runs.

"How is this different from hiring a ghostwriter?"

A ghostwriter writes your book and hands it to you. You still have to figure out how to sell it. The Pre-Sold Author writes your book and sells it while it is being written, using the podcast tour as both the content source and the marketing channel simultaneously. It is a book and a launch, not just a manuscript.

"What happens after the book is published?"

You own the book entirely. We provide a post-launch strategy guide for continuing to leverage podcast appearances to sell it. Many clients find that having a published book opens doors to higher-value podcast invitations, speaking opportunities, and client inquiries that compound over time.

"How much of my time does this actually take?"

During the 10–14 week tour phase, expect about 2–3 hours per week: one to two podcast interviews (45–60 minutes each) plus minimal prep time. We handle scheduling, coordination, and all production. In the architecture phase, you'll spend roughly 3–4 hours total with our team mapping your book. The editing phase requires almost nothing from you beyond one review of the manuscript. This is designed for busy professionals, not aspiring full-time writers.
