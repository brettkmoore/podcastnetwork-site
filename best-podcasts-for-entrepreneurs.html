Why Choosing the Right Show Matters

When you appear as a guest on a podcast, you're not just getting exposure. You're being introduced by a trusted voice to a specific audience. That introduction carries weight. If the host likes you and the audience likes you, listeners will visit your website, book calls with you, or buy your products.

But here's the problem most entrepreneurs face: they say yes to every podcast appearance, regardless of fit. They book a show with 50,000 downloads but the audience is mostly hobby entrepreneurs. They appear on another show with 5,000 downloads but zero audience overlap. The result? Lots of appearances, minimal actual business impact.

Strategic vs. Scattered Appearances

Scattered approach: 20 appearances on misaligned shows = 0 qualified leads and zero business growth, even though you reached 100,000+ people.

Strategic approach: 5 appearances on perfectly-aligned shows with 5,000-10,000 downloads each = 20-30 qualified leads and measurable business impact.

The strategic approach is what actually moves the needle. This is exactly why The Podcast Network exists—to help you skip the guesswork and get matched with shows where you'll actually win.

Podcast Categories to Target

1. Business Strategy & Growth Shows

These shows focus on building, scaling, and operationalizing businesses. Hosts interview entrepreneurs, founders, and operators about their growth strategies, fundraising, product development, and scaling tactics.

Ideal for: SaaS founders, agency owners, consultants, product creators

Typical audience: 5,000-50,000 downloads per episode

What they want: Specific growth stories, systems and frameworks, "how I scaled from X to Y"

2. Marketing & Sales Podcasts

These focus on go-to-market strategies, customer acquisition, sales techniques, and marketing frameworks. Hosts typically interview marketers, growth leaders, and sales experts.

Ideal for: Marketers, salespeople, customer success leaders, growth strategists

Typical audience: 3,000-20,000 downloads per episode

What they want: Conversion optimization tactics, customer acquisition stories, channel strategies

3. Leadership & Management Shows

These programs explore leadership philosophy, team building, organizational culture, and management practices. Hosts interview founders, executives, and management consultants.

Ideal for: Executives, HR leaders, organizational consultants, culture experts

Typical audience: 2,000-15,000 downloads per episode

What they want: Leadership lessons, team dynamics, scaling organizations, culture building

4. Mindset & Personal Development Shows

These podcasts explore mindset, resilience, discipline, habits, and personal growth. Hosts typically interview entrepreneurs, athletes, and high performers.

Ideal for: Coaches, therapists, habit experts, performance consultants

Typical audience: 5,000-100,000 downloads per episode

What they want: Inspiration, personal transformation stories, mindset frameworks

5. Industry-Specific Shows

These podcasts focus on specific industries (real estate, healthcare, finance, tech, etc.) and interview practitioners, leaders, and experts in that space.

Ideal for: Industry experts, niche consultants, specialists with deep expertise

Typical audience: 1,000-20,000 downloads per episode

What they want: Industry insights, specialized expertise, trend analysis

6. Niche & Community Shows

These are smaller, highly-engaged communities focused on specific audiences (women entrepreneurs, solopreneurs, bootstrapped founders, etc.). Hosts often interview people directly relevant to the community.

Ideal for: Anyone with a specific expertise or demographic focus

Typical audience: 500-5,000 downloads per episode (but highly engaged)

What they want: Direct relevance, accessibility, practical advice for their specific situation

How to Evaluate a Show Before You Pitch

Not every show is worth your time. Before pitching a show, evaluate it across these dimensions:

1. Audience Size & Growth

  • Downloads per episode: Target shows with 1,000+ downloads per episode. At minimum 500 for niche shows. Less than that and you're wasting your time on shows with no audience.
  • Growth trajectory: Is the show growing month-over-month? A show with consistent growth will have an increasingly valuable audience over time. A declining show is a waste of your platform.
  • Rank in category: Check if the show is Top 100 or Top 50 in its category. This indicates listenership quality.

2. Audience Fit

  • Demographics: Do the listeners match your ideal customer? If you sell to C-suite executives, a show with mostly junior employees isn't valuable.
  • Industry alignment: Does the show's audience work in industries where you can help?
  • Problem alignment: Do listeners have the problems you solve?

3. Host Quality & Engagement

  • Listening test: Listen to 2-3 recent episodes. Does the host ask thoughtful questions or just read a script? Is there genuine conversation or is it stilted?
  • Guest treatment: Are guests given time to share their message? Are they interrupted constantly? Are they set up to look good?
  • Audience engagement: Read reviews and comments. Do listeners engage? Do they trust the host's recommendations?

4. Production Quality

  • Audio quality: Is the sound clear and professional? Bad audio reflects poorly on you even if you sound good.
  • Editing: Is the show well-edited with good pacing? Or does it drag with long pauses and tangents?
  • Consistency: Does the show publish consistently? Inconsistent publishing suggests the host may not promote episodes well.

5. Guest Promotion & Visibility

  • Social amplification: Does the host post about guests on social media? Do they create clips?
  • Show notes quality: Are there detailed show notes with links? Poor show notes mean listeners can't easily find your links.
  • Guest spotlight: Does the host give you airtime to talk about your work or is it generic?

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find podcasts looking for guests in my niche?

The easiest way is using The Podcast Network's guest-matching platform. You create a profile with your expertise and talking points, and we match you with shows actively seeking guests. Alternatively, search podcast directories like Apple Podcasts and Spotify for keywords in your niche, then visit their websites or use tools like Podpage to find contact information. Look for shows that mention they're "looking for guests" or have a dedicated "guest booking" page.

What audience size should I target as a new guest?

As a first-time guest, aim for shows with 1,000-10,000 downloads per episode. These shows are large enough to provide meaningful exposure but typically more open to new voices than mega-shows (100,000+ downloads). Once you have 3-5 guest appearances under your belt, you can pitch larger shows. The Podcast Network matches you based on experience level, so we connect new guests with appropriate shows.

How important is audience overlap when choosing shows to pitch?

Audience overlap is critical. A show with 50,000 downloads per episode won't benefit your growth if the audience isn't aligned with your business. Focus on shows where at least 60-70% of listeners would be interested in your expertise. A show with 5,000 downloads to your ideal customer is worth 10 appearances on a 50,000-download show with a misaligned audience. The Podcast Network evaluates audience fit in our matching algorithm.

Should I pursue business podcasts or niche-specific podcasts?

Start with niche-specific podcasts where your expertise is directly relevant. These audiences are typically more engaged and more likely to take action on your recommendations. Once you build momentum and case studies, expand to broader business shows. A real estate agent benefits more from appearing on real estate investing podcasts than general entrepreneurship shows. Let the audience fit guide your selection.

What makes a podcast "good" for guest appearances?

Look for: (1) Host engagement - does the host ask thoughtful questions or just read a script? (2) Audience quality - engaged listeners who leave reviews and interact with content, (3) Guest integration - are guests introduced naturally and given time to share their message, or are they interruptions? (4) Production quality - clear audio, good editing, and professional appearance, (5) Growth trajectory - is the show growing month-over-month? (6) Audience alignment - listeners match your ideal customer profile.
